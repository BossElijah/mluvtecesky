The accusative plural (4th case plural) - simplified rules for the nouns

You can use simplified rules for the accusative plural of the nouns. These rules do not work 100% of the time but they can help you in common practice.

rule ending
1. Most nouns -y
2. The nouns ending in the consonant with "háček" (hook) (ž, š, č, ř, ď, ť, ň), c, j and -e/-ě -e/-ě
3. Neuter nouns ending in -o -a

Attention:

  1. Some nouns have stem changes in their declined forms (pes – psi, dům – domy).
  2. Some nouns have irregular forms (člověk – lidé/lidi, dítě – děti).
  3. The neuter nouns of the type nádraží do not change in the accusative plural. The neuter nouns of the type kuře have a special declension, e.g. in the accusative plural kuřata.
  4. The feminine nouns of the type místnost have the ending -i in the accusative plural.
