The nominative plural (1st case plural) - simplified rules for the nouns

You can use simplified rules for the nominative plural of the nouns. These rules do not work 100% of the time but they can help you in common practice.

rule ending
1. Most nouns -y
2. The nouns ending in the consonant with "háček" (= a hook) (ž, š, č, ř, ď, ť, ň), c, j and -e/-ě -e/-ě
3. The neuter nouns ending in -o -a
Attention: Ma -i

Attention:

  1. Some nouns have stem alternations in their declined forms (pes – psi, dům – domy).
  2. Some nouns have irregular forms (člověk – lidé/lidi, dítě – děti).
  3. The neuter nouns of the type nádraží do not change in the nominative plural. The neuter nouns of the type kuře have a special declension, e.g. in the nominative plural kuřata.
  4. The feminine nouns of the type místnost have the ending -i in the nominative plural.
  5. In the masculine animate nouns, some consonants are softened, for example doktor – doktoři, kluk – kluci.
  6. Some masculine animate nouns have endings -é or -ové: the masculine animate nouns ending in -tel and some other words have the ending -é (e.g. učitelé, Španělé). The borrowed masculine animate nouns ending in -f, -g, -l, -m and some short masculine animate nouns have the ending -ové (e.g. kolegové, fotografové, Rusové). Notice the endings -é and -ové sometimes alternate with the ending -i, for example Angličani/Angličané, Švédi/Švédové.
