1 Case. Notice the nominative is called "1st case", the genitive "2nd case", the dative "3rd case" etc. in the Czech terminology.

2 The Demonstrative Pronouns ten, ta, to. Alike ten, ta, to also tento, tato, toto and tenhle, tahle, tohle are declined.

The possessive pronouns můj, moje. Alike můj, moje the pronouns tvůj, svůj, náš, váš are declined: the endings are added to the forms {tv-, sv-} without „m“ (e.g. m-ých > tv-ých, sv-ých but mind naš-ich, vaš-ich). The pronouns můj, tvůj have in some cases also short literary forms (e.g. mí, mé, má, tví, tvé, tvá) which are declined like the adjective nový. The pronoun její is declined like the adjective moderní. The possessive pronouns jeho, jejich are indeclinable. The pronoun svůj, svoje which is declined like můj, moje is used in standard Czech if something belongs to the person expressed by a subject in the sentence. Compare: Hledám mé (= non standard)/své (= standard) studenty.

3 The Adjectives. The nominative singular form of the adjectives can be ending in -ý (-ý adjectives, hard adjectives) or in -í (-í adjectives, soft adjectives). The declensions of these adjectives are different.

4 Nouns. In the 2nd declension group, notice the same ending for six paradigms in the accusative and locative plural.

Attention: Consonant alternations happen in the declined forms of some nouns and adjectives: -ů → -o (dům → domy), the floating -e- (den → dny) a softening of -k → -ci (kluk → kluci, hezký → hezcí), -r → -ři (doktor → doktoři)...

* Some masculine animate nouns (often the proper names) and exceptionally also masculine inanimate nouns ending in -l, -s, -z, -x (e.g. král, cíl, Klaus, Francouz, Felix) belong to the 2nd declension group and they are declined like the paradigm muž/počítač.

** Several masculine nouns ending in -tel, -c (e.g. hotel, kostel, tác) belong to the 1st declension group. Some feminine nouns ending in a consonant belong to the 3rd declension group (very often ending in -ost, -st, see the paradigm místnost). But: some feminine nouns (e.g. věc, řeč) are declined like místnost and others (e.g. moc, pomoc, nemoc, noc, myš, smrt, sůl, paměť, zeď, odpověď, loď) vary between the paradigms kancelář and místnost.

*** A small group of neuter nouns ending in -e/-ě which denote some animals (zvíře, prase, kuře...) and food (rajče) has a special declension. In plural: 1. kuřata, 2. kuřat, 3. kuřatům, 4. kuřata, 5. kuřata!, 6. kuřatech, 7. kuřaty. Few masculine animate nouns ending in -e (e.g. soudce) are declined like the paradigm muž in plural.
